Hi!, welcome to my personal website.
My name is Allan. I am an Economist with a bachelor's degree from the Faculty of Economics at the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM). Currently, I work as a Junior Researcher at the University Program of Development Studies (PUED) and as a Research Assistant at the Postgraduate Unit of Economics at UNAM. Additionally, at the Faculty of Economics, I serve as a Teaching Assistant for Econometrics I and II, where I primarily lead the R programming laboratories.

At the Postgraduate Unit of Economics, I work alongside Dr. Arturo Huerta González. My research lies at the intersection of heterodox economics, and monetary and fiscal policy oriented toward growth and economic development.  Post-Keynesian economics and their application to public policies aimed at addressing poverty and inequality. At PUED, I collaborate with Dr. Delfino Vargas Chanes, focusing on the integration of remote sensing techniques (satellite imagery) into socioeconomic research. My recent work involves applying these tools to measure multidimensional poverty and analyze labor informality. I have also investigated remote sensing applications to monitor Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) compliance in countries with limited statistical coverage.

My mission is to establish myself as a policy-maker and researcher with a high level of incidence in strategic decision-making on social policies. My primary objective is the design of evidence-based strategies to eradicate extreme poverty in Mexico through sustainable growth. I am convinced that the world’s challenges require shared solutions. Therefore, I seek to foster technical and human collaboration networks among researchers and activists throughout the entire region to develop projects that advance the 2030 Agenda. If you share this interest, please connect with me via LinkedIn.
