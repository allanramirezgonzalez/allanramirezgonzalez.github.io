Hi!, welcome to my personal website.
My name is Allan. I am an Economist with a bachelor's degree from the Faculty of Economics at the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM). Currently, I serve as a Junior Researcher at the University Program of Development Studies (PUED) and as a Research Assistant at the Postgraduate Unit of Economics at UNAM. Also, I am a Teaching Assistant for Econometrics I and II, where I lead the R programming laboratories.

At the Postgraduate Unit of Economics, I collaborate with Dr. Arturo Huerta González. My research focuses on Post-Keynesian economics to design monetary and fiscal policy proposals oriented toward growth, economic development, and their application to public policies aimed at reducing poverty and inequality. At PUED, I work alongside Dr. Delfino Vargas Chanes, integrating remote sensing techniques and satellite imagery into socioeconomic research. My recent work applies these tools to measure multidimensional poverty, analyze labor informality, and monitor Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) compliance in regions with limited statistical coverage.

My mission is to establish myself as a policy-maker and researcher with a high level of incidence in strategic decision-making. I am committed to designing evidence-based strategies to eradicate extreme poverty in Mexico through sustainable growth. I am convinced that today’s global challenges require shared, multidisciplinary solutions. Therefore, I am actively seeking to expand my network of collaborations to generate projects that help achieve the 2030 Agenda. I aim to foster technical and human networks among researchers and activists across the region to advance these goals. If you share these interests or wish to explore potential synergies, please connect with me via LinkedIn.
