I am an economist from the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM). Currently, I work as a Research Assistant at the University Program of Development Studies (PUED) and the Postgraduate Unit of Economics at UNAM. I also serve as a Teaching Assistant for the Econometrics course, leading the R programming labs.

My research lies at the intersection of heterodox economics, environmental sustainability, and social equity. I have a strong interest in Modern Monetary Theory (MMT) and Post-Keynesian economics, and their application to public policy development aimed at addressing poverty and inequality.

Methodologically, I focus on integrating data science, R programming, and remote sensing techniques (satellite imagery) into socioeconomic research. My recent work involves applying these tools to measure multidimensional poverty and analyze labor informality.

You can find my complete CV [here](/cv/) and explore my recent publications and working papers in the respective sections above.
