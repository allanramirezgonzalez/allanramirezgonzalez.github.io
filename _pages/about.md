Hi!, welcome to my personal website.
My name is Allan. I am an Economist with a bachelor's degree from the Faculty of Economics at the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM).

Currently, I work as a Junior Researcher at the University Program of Development Studies (PUED) and as a Research Assistant at the Postgraduate Unit of Economics at UNAM. Additionally, at the Faculty of Economics, I serve as a Teaching Assistant for Econometrics I and II, where I primarily lead the R programming laboratories.

At the Postgraduate Unit of Economics, I work alongside Dr. Arturo Huerta González. My research lies at the intersection of heterodox economics, and monetary and fiscal policy oriented toward growth and economic development. I have a strong interest in Modern Monetary Theory (MMT) and Post-Keynesian economics, particularly their application to public policies aimed at addressing poverty and inequality.

At PUED, I collaborate with Dr. Delfino Vargas Chanes, focusing on the integration of data science, R programming, and remote sensing techniques (satellite imagery) into socioeconomic research. My recent work involves applying these tools to measure multidimensional poverty and analyze labor informality. I have also investigated remote sensing applications to monitor Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) compliance in countries with limited statistical coverage.

You can find my complete CV [here](/cv/) and explore my recent publications and working papers in the respective sections above.
